Focus Use Case 1

AI-optimised 4G District Heating

At a Glance

Positioning:

Daily operation (dispatch, temps) and medium-term decisions (4G conversion, storage) for heat and cooling networks.

Veolia-specific context:

Highly instrumented networks (Poland, Czech) with digital twins; growing portfolio of complex 4G/5G systems (e.g., Saclay).

Core processes in scope

  • Network operation: Dispatch between production units (CHP, biomass, WtE, gas, geothermal, HPs, data centre heat), management of temperatures/pressures, handling constraints.
  • Planning and asset decisions: Definition of 4G conversion trajectories, integration of new sources, dimensioning of new assets and extensions.

Veolia’s current situation

  • In CEE, Veolia has high telemetry penetration and operational digital twins.
  • In Western Europe, the situation is more heterogeneous; some networks are advanced, others rely on traditional tools.
  • Operation and planning are mostly network-by-network, with limited systematic cross-fertilisation of best practices.

Typical pain points observed

  • Dispatch decisions depend heavily on local experience, not explicit portfolio-level optimisation of cost, CO₂, and risk.
  • Difficult to compare performance between networks and identify what “good” looks like in a 4G environment.
  • 4G business cases are built with static models, making it hard to test multiple scenarios.

AI ambition for this use case

  • Add an AI layer on top of existing digital twins and SCADA to:
  • Propose optimised dispatch and temperature strategies under multiple scenarios (prices, weather, demand, CO₂ costs).
  • Simulate 4G conversion and new source integration, providing robust inputs for capex decisions.
  • Standardise a set of “4G operating playbooks” that can be shared across markets.

Value levers for Veolia

  • Production cost optimisation: Better sequencing of units; increased utilisation of low-cost sources (waste heat, WtE).
  • Network performance: Optimised temperatures/pressures to reduce thermal losses.
  • Capex and 4G planning: Use AI-enhanced twins to test 4G scenarios dynamically.
  • Operational robustness: Capture and codify best practices, reducing dependence on a few experts.

KPIs to track

  • €/MWh produced and delivered; CO₂/MWh delivered; % renewable/recovered heat.
  • % of hours operated at low temperature; network loss ratio; incident rates.
  • NPV and payback of 4G/asset conversion projects.

CVA’s contribution

  • Programme framing: Map and segment Veolia’s network portfolio by data readiness, challenge, and value. Design the "4G AI cockpit" concept.
  • CORE-based design: Apply CORE to the end-to-end “heat supply” process to identify where AI insights should be embedded.
  • SmartPMO to manage scale up: Build a portfolio view to ensure each local project contributes to a coherent, group-level 4G programme.

Focus Use Case 2

AI Orchestrator for Local Loops

At a Glance

Positioning:

Design, operation, and governance of local multi-energy systems (heat, buildings, DCs, DER, storage, demand response).

Veolia-specific context:

Existing "Local Loops" vision, pilot projects, and unique combination of Hubgrade, Flexcity, and water/waste assets at a district level.

Core processes in scope

  • Local system design: Defining architecture, sizing assets, and setting contractual boundaries (Veolia, city, industrials, DCs, residents).
  • Daily/seasonal orchestration: Optimising flows (WtE vs. DC heat), using storage, and activating electrical flexibility via Flexcity.
  • Governance and value sharing: Allocation of costs and benefits (tariffs, rebates, revenue shares).

Veolia’s current situation

  • Veolia has a clear vision and emblematic projects. Flexcity and Hubgrade provide working platforms.
  • However, each loop is largely a custom project. Replication is possible but not yet industrialised.

Pain points & strategic opportunities

  • High transaction costs and long lead times to structure each loop (design, legal, stakeholders).
  • Difficulty for non-specialists to trust the value sharing in complex local systems.
  • Risk of being pre-empted by competitors on flagship projects if Veolia cannot demonstrate a scalable methodology.

AI ambition for this use case

  • Develop an AI-assisted “Local Loop orchestrator” that:
  • Ingests local data and generates optimised loop designs and run strategies.
  • Produces transparent value breakdowns and sensitivities for partners (cities, DCs).
  • Supports continuous optimisation once operational, including Flexcity activation.

Value levers at loop level

  • Total energy cost reduction: Co-optimise heat, cold, and electricity; reduce peak imports via storage/demand response.
  • Increased monetisation: Turn loop flexibilities (industrial loads, DC backups) into new revenues via Flexcity.
  • More efficient capex: Use AI scenarios to test alternative architectures before investing.
  • Higher credibility: Provide transparent, data-based evidence to support approvals and de-risk complex PPPs.

KPIs to track

  • Total cost of energy €/MWh vs. reference; CO₂/MWh; % local/recovered energy.
  • Gross/net revenues from flexibility; CAPEX per unit of demand decarbonised.
  • Stakeholder satisfaction and perceived fairness.

CVA’s contribution

  • Formalise the Local Loop economic model: Build standard economic templates for different loop archetypes (urban, industrial, DC-heavy).
  • CORE-based governance design: Map the end-to-end decision chain (from city strategy to tariffs) and embed AI outputs to support negotiations.
  • Integration with Veolia’s platforms: Specify how the AI orchestrator sits with Hubgrade, Flexcity, and asset control systems.
  • Programme and scaling view: Prioritise 2–3 pilot loops and design a replication playbook, using SmartPMO to track value.

Focus Use Case 3

AI-enhanced Hubgrade Cockpit

At a Glance

Positioning:

Monitoring and optimisation of thousands of building/industrial sites under EPCs. Maintenance and lifecycle management of on-site energy systems.

Veolia-specific context:

Hubgrade centres already aggregate data, but practices are heterogeneous and scaling is resource-intensive.

Core processes in scope

  • Energy performance management: Monitoring consumption, comfort, and KPIs across thousands of sites; identifying drifts and triggering corrective actions.
  • Maintenance and lifecycle: Planning preventive maintenance and managing interventions on boilers, chillers, HVAC, etc., under OPEX/performance guarantees.

Veolia’s current situation

  • Hubgrade provides centralised supervision with live dashboards, alerts, and rule-based optimisation.
  • Practices, tools, and performance remain heterogeneous across countries.
  • Scaling high performance is increasingly resource-intensive as the number of sites grows.

Pain points & improvement opportunities

  • Difficulty prioritising actions: which buildings are the biggest value pockets? Which equipment is at highest risk?
  • Limited systematisation of learning; similar buildings don't benefit from each other's experience.
  • Performance reporting to clients is time-consuming and not always focused on cross-sell/upsell levers.

AI ambition for this use case

  • Build an AI-enhanced Hubgrade cockpit that:
  • Scores sites and equipment by potential value and risk (energy, CO₂, incident).
  • Recommends and sequences actions (parameter changes, audits, maintenance) with quantified impact.
  • Predicts future drifts and failures to move from reactive to proactive management.
  • Generates sharper performance narratives to support client discussions and upsell.

Value levers for Veolia

  • Improved portfolio performance: Use AI to scan the portfolio and highlight the 10-20% of sites with the most savings potential.
  • Reduced downtime: Predict failures of critical assets to optimise maintenance.
  • Better client conversations: Support QBRs with AI-generated insights and quantified performance stories.
  • Upsell: Identify retrofit/service opportunities based on site-specific AI diagnostics.

KPIs to track

  • % reduction in energy/CO₂ vs. baseline; number/severity of unplanned outages.
  • Productivity: sites per Hubgrade analyst, actions implemented per month.
  • Contract renewal rate, upsell conversion rate, margin improvement.

CVA’s contribution

  • CORE "Hubgrade-to-Value" Map: Map how Hubgrade creates value (data -> decision -> action -> outcome) and identify AI augmentation points.
  • AI Use Case Design: Define and prioritise a limited set of AI modules (site scoring, anomaly detection, action recommendation).
  • SmartPMO & Change: Build governance to deploy AI modules in waves. Support change so staff see AI as an amplifier, not a black box.

CORE® and DIDA®

Our reference framework for AI-enabled process redesign.

What CORE® is: A Proven Methodology

An integrated methodology on three levels

  • User / client journeys: Design target journeys from the user’s point of view (VoC, service standards).
  • Data flows: Optimise the data model needed to steer satisfaction, cost and carbon end-to-end.
  • Processes: Rebuild processes in a “data-native” way, rethinking decisions and embedding AI where it creates value.

Organised in 2 Lots and 5 Modules

  • Lot 1 – Baseline and redesign (data-native):
    • M1 – E2E diagnostic and baselines (satisfaction, efficiency, carbon).
    • M2 – Target journeys.
    • M3 – Target DIDA® flows.
  • Lot 2 – Gap analysis and transformation path:
    • M4 – Process impacts and levers.
    • M5 – Transformation trajectory and “base camps”.

What DIDA® is: The Bridge Between Business and AI

DIDA® is the core protocol that connects journeys, data, and processes. It decomposes each journey step into four elements, forcing clarity on how AI changes decisions and actions.

D – Data

List the data needed to monitor and deliver the journey step; identify and collect all accessible sources automatically.

I – Insights

Use analytics and AI to transform raw data into insights that are truly useful for decision-making.

D – Decision

Define how decisions will be taken (rules, models, exceptions) based on these insights.

A – Action

Build a process and, where relevant, automation that executes the decision or augments an employee to act effectively.

Why this matters for an AI for Energy programme: The three AI use cases are process-heavy. CORE® and DIDA® bring a rigorous, battle-tested way to map these processes, identify the exact roles of data and AI, and design target, AI-enabled processes that teams can implement.
