European DHN market is driven by clear regulatory trends, strong decarbonization momentum, and significant investment opportunities (2025-2030)

Germany, UK, France, Spain, Poland, Czech Republic, and Hungary represent distinct market structures, offering clearly differentiated investment opportunities totaling up to ~â‚¬36bn, driven by Greenfield (GF) development in Western Europe and Brownfield (BF) retrofits in Eastern Europe

Key market trends and characteristics

Western Europe (Germany, UK, France, Spain)

  • Primarily driven by GF 4G/5G DH network developments, supported by zoning regulation and strong subsidy programs (Germany: BEW/KfW, UK: GHNF/HNIP, France: Fonds Chaleur, Spain: NextGen EU)
  • Limited retrofit opportunities due to recent installed bases (UK, Spain) or fragmented private ownership and limited economic incentives (Germany BF)
  • PPP/private merchant models prevalent; France dominated by public service delegation (PSD) concessions, highly competitive but stable and predictable regulatory environment

Eastern Europe (Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary)

  • Driven by large-scale BF retrofit opportunities due to mandatory coal phase-outs and significant modernization needs
  • Substantial EU Modernization Fund subsidies (up to ~70% CAPEX) covering coal-to-renewable conversions
  • Municipally-owned assets dominating, with capped regulated returns but clear trend towards concession renewals and openness to private capital (particularly Czech Republic, Hungary)
  • Poland represents largest BF volume but structurally closed market dominated by municipal/state incumbents, limiting external growth
